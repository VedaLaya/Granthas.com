    Characters

    Characters from Mahabharata

    • Arjuna

      The protagonist of the Bhagavad Gita, Arjuna embodies qualities of humility, introspection, devotion, and courage. His willingness to learn and his inner conflict serve as a catalyst for the teachings of the Gita.

    • Bhishma

      A revered elder and commander-in-chief of the Kuru army, Bhishma is known for his unwavering commitment to duty, integrity, and righteousness. Despite being on the opposing side of the battlefield.

    • Dronacharya

      The royal guru and teacher of both the Pandavas and the Kauravas, Dronacharya symbolizes discipline, knowledge, and loyalty. His dedication to imparting martial skills and moral values to his students reflects his noble character.

    • Yudhishthira

      The eldest of the Pandava brothers, Yudhishthira personifies virtues such as righteousness, honesty, and integrity. He is known for his unwavering commitment to Dharma (righteousness) and his adherence to truth, even in the face of adversity.

    • Sanjaya

      The charioteer of King Dhritarashtra and narrator of the Bhagavad Gita, Sanjaya exhibits qualities of wisdom, perception, and empathy. He provides insightful commentary on the events of the battlefield, demonstrating his keen understanding of human nature and spiritual truths.

    • Kripacharya

      Another respected teacher and warrior, Kripacharya is known for his loyalty, wisdom, and martial prowess. He serves as a mentor to both the Pandavas and the Kauravas, offering guidance and support in times of need.
